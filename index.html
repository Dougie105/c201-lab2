Lab 2: About me...

My name is Ellen Conley. I grew up mostly in Iowa living with my mom during the school year, spending summers in Northern California with my dad. After high school, I moved to Seattle to go to UW. Back then I thought I wanted to be a doctor, so I did all of my pre-med coursework, worked in a dementia research lab at the VA Hospital, and volunteered for a student-run free medical clinic. I really loved the humanities classes I took though, so I ended up majoring in International Studies and never ended up doing much with all the math and science I took.

After college, I decided to do Americorps instead of apply to medical school, and that kind of set me on a path to spend my twenties doing college access work. After a few years of that, I felt a little stuck and regretted not pursuing a more technical career, but I hadn't taken enough math and science to do a STEM grad program. So, I ended up doing my Master of Public Administration and found that I loved my quantitative analysis courses and that I really really loved manipulating data and learning programming languages. I ended up getting pretty good at STATA and R in grad school, and then I learned SAS and SQL working as a data analyst last year.

As data analyst without a PhD or STEM degree though, I felt like once I became a good programmer, I'd probably plateau; and the money I was making wasn't great. So, I'm here! My goal is to get a job as a developer or engineer at a company with a feminist culture, where I'll have good mentors, and where there could be opportunities to collaborate with engineers in other STEM fields.
